abstract: |-
  Conventional emotional TTS methods rely on **discrete emotion labels** to control emotion category and intensity, which fails to capture the complexity and continuity of human emotional perception and expression. Furthermore, large-scale speech corpora with balanced emotion distribution and fine-grained annotation are scarce, causing synthesis models to over-fit and obstructing effective emotion control. We propose **UDDETTS**, a unified framework that **integrates discrete and dimensional emotions**. UDDETTS introduces the interpretable **Arousal–Dominance–Valence (ADV)** space for dimensional emotion description and supports emotion control driven by either discrete labels or non-linearly quantized ADV values. By disentangling speech emotion along three psychologically meaningful dimensions, UDDETTS achieves **fine-grained, linear 3-D emotion controllability**. Experimental results show UDDETTS significantly outperforms baselines in naturalness and emotion-control accuracy, demonstrating the superiority of the ADV space for end-to-end emotional TTS.
